About Makhdoom Talib-ul-Mola

(4 October 1919 – 11 January 1993)

Makhdoom Muhammad Zaman Talib-ul-Mola was a Pakistani politician, scholar, and celebrated Sindhi poet. As the 17th Sajjadah Nasheen of Ghous-ul-Haq Makhdoom Sarwar Nooh Shrine — famously known as "Sarwari Jammat" in Hala — he became Sajjadah Nasheen after the death of his father Makhdoom Ghulam Muhammad. His shrine is famous as "No Lakhi Godri."

He was a founding member of Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP), which was founded in his house at Hala in 1967. He served as Senior Vice Chairman of PPP, was elected Member of the National Assembly multiple times, and played a key role in the MRD movement and anti-One Unit movement.

As Chairman of Sindhi Adabi Board, he authored more than 30 books of poetry and prose in Sindhi, Persian, Urdu, and Siraiki. He was awarded Hilal-e-Imtiaz, Tamgha-e-Pakistan, and Latif Award for his contributions.
